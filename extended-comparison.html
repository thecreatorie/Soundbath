Patterns Across All Five

0/5
None have a community platform. Not one of the five bathhouses — luxury or boutique — has a digital community layer. No Circle.so, no Discord, no member portal. Guests interact at the property and disconnect digitally. This is unoccupied whitespace across the entire US market.
0/5
None have a structured CRM spine. Mindbody, Meevo, and Tock track transactions, not relationships. No operator has confirmed HubSpot-level contact lifecycle tracking — source attribution, journey stage, referral origination. SOUNDBATH would be the first in its class with this infrastructure.
0/5
None have a pre-launch founding member email pipeline. Every operator in this set was building an audience after opening, not before. Beehiiv gives SOUNDBATH a tool none of these businesses used — a newsletter-first founding member cultiviation strategy before doors open.
2/5
Two rely on Squarespace — the small operators. Watershed and Onsen both use Squarespace. Both are single-location boutiques. Every operator at SOUNDBATH's target scale and luxury tier (AIRE, QC NY) uses custom or proprietary systems. Framer positions SOUNDBATH at that tier without the custom build cost.
1/5
Only one has a meaningful membership program. Remedy Place's Residency tiers are the strongest membership model in the peer set. But even that runs through Mindbody with no CRM integration. SOUNDBATH's Glofox + HubSpot pipeline gives the membership model a data backbone Remedy Place doesn't have.
0/5
None have a referral-to-booking pipeline. Remedy Place has an Insiders discount program for professionals — not guest-to-guest social referral. No competitor has built infrastructure that turns existing guests into a direct acquisition channel. SOUNDBATH's Beehiiv referral loop can own this entirely.
Stack Recommendation · Based on Extended Benchmark Analysis
The Experience Stack.
Five properties. Three patterns. One clear answer for a social-forward Miami launch.
  • 1
    Community is the biggest gap in the entire market — and it's the core of SOUNDBATH's brief. Zero out of five competitors have a digital community layer. Circle.so is in the Experience Stack. The Architect Stack doesn't include it by default. For a brand whose brief explicitly calls for "social-forward," deploying the only community platform in the US bathhouse market is the single clearest competitive move available.
  • 2
    No competitor has a pre-launch pipeline. Beehiiv gives SOUNDBATH a window that doesn't exist twice. The founding member moment is a one-time event. Every operator in this analysis had to build their audience after opening. Beehiiv (Experience Stack) is purpose-built for the pre-launch phase — referral mechanics, subscriber growth, founding member sequencing. The Architect Stack replaces this with HubSpot email sequences, which are powerful post-launch but less optimized for the viral, newsletter-native founding member playbook.
  • 3
    Framer matches the brand tier of AIRE and QC NY — without the custom build cost or timeline. Squarespace is for Watershed and Onsen. AIRE and QC NY built custom. Framer sits between them: a premium, React-output brand site that can launch in weeks, not quarters. The Architect Stack's Webflow is capable but leans CMS-first — better for content-heavy sites than brand-immersive launch experiences.
  • 4
    HubSpot CRM is in both stacks — SOUNDBATH gets the data advantage either way. Both stacks close the CRM gap that every competitor has. This isn't a differentiator between the two stacks; it's a differentiator against the market. The Experience Stack gets there faster because Glofox and Beehiiv have tighter native HubSpot integration paths than Zenoti and Webflow.
  • 5
    Glofox is the right booking tool for SOUNDBATH's model — not Mindbody, not Squarespace, not Tock. Remedy Place uses Mindbody (class-scheduling-first). Watershed uses Meevo (full-service spa). Onsen uses Tock (restaurant-native timed entry). None of those are optimal for a social membership bathhouse with open-circuit access. Glofox is built for wellness membership businesses with branded app access and member tiers. It's the closest category fit in this peer set.
Where the Architect Stack still has merit

If SOUNDBATH's owners are certain they will expand to multiple locations within 3 years, the Architect Stack's Stripe billing ownership and Webflow CMS portability become more valuable over time. Every operator that scaled to 10+ locations (AIRE, QC Terme) eventually built proprietary systems — the Architect Stack builds those foundations earlier.

The Experience Stack is the right choice for winning the Miami launch. If multi-city expansion is on the roadmap within 24 months, revisit the Architect Stack's billing architecture in Phase 2 before locking into long-term Glofox contracts. The two stacks are not incompatible — you can evolve from one to the other.
