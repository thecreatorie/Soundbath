The Architect Stack

A modular architecture where HubSpot owns the customer relationship, Stripe owns billing, and Zenoti owns operations — designed for a social-forward bathhouse that wants to own its customer data at every layer and integrate smoothly as the stack grows. More configuration upfront than the Experience Stack, but the data architecture survives any platform change.

DNS & infrastructure
Cloudflare
DNS Primary pick

Best DNS management available. Free CDN, DDoS protection, DNSSEC, and SSL. Centralized management for all domains, redirects, and email authentication. Supports domain transfers at cost.

Google Workspace
Email

Business Starter. Set SPF first, then DKIM — DMARC cannot be configured without one of these in place. Key aliases: hello@, members@, bookings@, press@. Never send outbound email before all three records are active.

Website & waitlist capture
Webflow
Landing page + CMS Primary pick

Strong editorial design control and a mature CMS — better than Framer for a site that will grow to many content pages over time. Steeper initial learning curve but more depth at scale. Note: Webflow pricing has two components — site plan and workspace plan — verify both before purchasing.

HubSpot Forms
Waitlist capture Primary pick

Embed native HubSpot forms directly in Webflow. Signups go straight into HubSpot contacts with source attribution — no Zapier needed for the core waitlist flow. Simpler than Beehiiv, but loses referral mechanics and newsletter capability unless built manually.

CRM & data foundation
HubSpot CRM
CRM spine Primary pick

The relationship system of record for the entire stack. Handles waitlist, segmented lists, lifecycle stages, press contacts, and long-term member history. Free CRM covers up to 1M contacts. Starter plan removes branding and unlocks basic automation. This is the non-negotiable tool — everything else feeds here.

Google Shared Drives
Cloud storage

Shared Drives (not personal folders) ensure the business retains file ownership as the team grows. Structure: /Brand /Operations /Legal /Finance /Real Estate /Marketing /Programming /Membership /Vendors /Buildout.

Airtable
Ops database (optional)

Useful internal workspace for vendor tracking, programming planning, and buildout timelines. Not the customer database — HubSpot owns that. Use Airtable only for internal operational tracking that doesn't fit HubSpot.

Zapier / Make
Integration

Early integration glue. This stack uses fewer native integrations than the Experience Stack, so Zapier carries more connection work. Graduate to direct API webhooks when volume justifies it.

Membership billing
Stripe Billing
Billing platform Primary pick

Source of truth for recurring memberships, stored payment methods, promo codes, and founding offers. Stripe's subscription model scales without architectural changes. Billing history is fully portable — stays with Stripe regardless of which ops platform is chosen at Phase 3. Requires real developer configuration — not a plug-and-play solution.

HubSpot (member layer)
Member CRM

Member lifecycle management, engagement segmentation, referral tracking, and all communications live in HubSpot. Stripe feeds billing events here via webhook. The architectural key: billing in Stripe, relationships in HubSpot — neither depends on the other.

Events & programming
Eventbrite
Event ticketing Primary recommendation

Mature API and webhook model with strong operational defaults. Eventbrite's marketplace exposes SOUNDBATH events to millions of potential attendees. Better long-term data infrastructure and HubSpot integration path. Right choice if operational maturity matters more than social aesthetics.

Luma
Event ticketing (alternative)

Social-first and a better fit for Miami's creative scene. Lower blended fees than Eventbrite at comparable volume. Consider if brand aesthetics and community discovery matter more than API depth. The Architect Stack can swap Eventbrite for Luma without changing the rest of the architecture. See the comparison page for the full breakdown.

Community
HubSpot email sequences
Community communications

Automated email sequences, segmented lists, and lifecycle-based messaging form the community layer in this stack. Functional and efficient — but one-directional. Members receive information, they don't participate in discussions.

⚠ For a brand built on social immersion, the absence of Circle.so limits the pre-opening community story. Adding Circle.so doesn't break the Architect Stack's data model — it sits alongside HubSpot and Stripe without replacing either. Strongly consider including it.

Circle.so (optional add)
Community platform (optional)

Circle.so can sit alongside HubSpot and Stripe without architectural conflict. Sync Circle membership events to HubSpot via Zapier. A transaction fee applies to payments processed through Circle — plan membership payment flows accordingly. For SOUNDBATH's social brand positioning, this is worth serious consideration even in the Architect Stack.

Referral-to-booking pipeline

The original brief specifically calls out a "referral / guest booking system" — meaning a flow where a referred person can not only join the waitlist but book a visit or founding membership directly. In this stack, the referral mechanics and the booking endpoint are handled separately, which gives more flexibility but requires deliberate wiring.

Referral source is captured at the point of contact creation in HubSpot — via a UTM-tagged landing page link or a unique referral code embedded in the URL. Every referred contact has a clear source attribution from day one. This is the cleanest referral tracking approach available without a dedicated referral platform.

Stripe referral-to-booking

A referred person follows a referral link to a Stripe-powered membership checkout or an Eventbrite/Luma booking page. Stripe promo codes can give referred users a discount or trial offer — this is the booking endpoint. Stripe logs the conversion, a webhook fires to HubSpot, and the referral chain is closed in the CRM. No dedicated referral platform needed in early stages.

Email authentication order

SPF first, then DKIM — DMARC depends on at least one. All records in Cloudflare DNS. Never send outbound email before all three are active. Poor authentication causes deliverability failures that are difficult to reverse.

The three-system ownership model: HubSpot owns the master relationship record. Stripe owns the billing relationship. Zenoti owns operational visit and service records. Changing any one platform doesn't compromise the customer relationship in the other two — this is the fundamental advantage of this architecture.
