🔍
How this analysis was conducted. Each competitor's website was fetched directly and its source code inspected for technology fingerprints — CDN URLs, script tags, noscript notices, booking subdomain patterns, and third-party pixel tags. These signals were cross-referenced against public databases (ZoomInfo, Wappalyzer methodology), press coverage, and job listings. Findings are labelled: Confirmed (directly observable in source), Inferred (strongly implied by observable signals), or Unknown (not determinable from public data). No private data, no speculation presented as fact.

Business model

Business model

Othership runs a class-based model with guided and free-flow sessions at set times — closer to a fitness studio than a traditional bathhouse in terms of scheduling. Drop-in passes, multi-visit packs, and monthly memberships are the three product tiers. Sessions are 75–120 minutes, fully programmed with music, breathwork, and essential oils, led by trained guides. The alcohol-free, body-positive, emotionally expressive culture is the brand differentiator and creates strong community loyalty. The breathwork app (separate from the booking app) extends the brand into digital wellness — a meaningful second revenue and engagement channel. The NYC expansion (Flatiron, Williamsburg) following the Toronto proof-of-concept shows a clear and working scaling playbook.

Mariana Tek is the key finding. It's a boutique fitness studio platform purpose-built for class scheduling, member management, and branded apps. Othership is using it at scale across 4 locations. Perkville is a loyalty/referral platform used widely in fitness studios.
Mariana Tek — what it is and why it matters

Mariana Tek is a boutique fitness platform used by premium studio brands (Equinox, SoulCycle, CorePower, and now Othership). It handles class scheduling, membership management, drop-in purchases, guest check-in, a branded mobile app, and a referral system — all under one platform. It's the industry standard for class-based wellness businesses. This is the tool Othership chose instead of Glofox or Mindbody. For SOUNDBATH's Experience Stack, Mariana Tek deserves evaluation alongside Glofox — especially if SOUNDBATH will run programmed classes or guided sessions rather than purely open-access entry.

Strategic strengths
  • Category-defining brand in social sauna — the benchmark for the experience-led wellness movement
  • Proven multi-city scaling playbook: Toronto → NYC works, now expanding further
  • Mariana Tek gives them professional class scheduling, branded app, and multi-location ops from day one
  • Perkville referral program with 22% incentive is measurably driving community growth
  • Breathwork app creates a second revenue channel and daily engagement touchpoint outside the physical space
  • 1,000+ five-star reviews — social proof machine with strong community identity
  • Alcohol-free, body-positive culture generates press and loyalty simultaneously
Gaps and weaknesses
  • Class-based scheduling creates a harder access model than open-circuit bathhouse entry — less spontaneous
  • No F&B or restaurant component — revenue ceiling lower than full bathhouse model
  • No cultural programming layer (talks, residencies, art) — wellness-first, culture-second
  • Webflow + Mariana Tek is effective but offers limited data ownership — CRM layer not visible
  • Shopify as a separate subdomain signals disconnected retail and core operations data
  • No RFID or locker-integrated payments visible — likely simpler locker model
  • Not in Miami — no local market intelligence or brand presence here yet
Implications for SOUNDBATH

Othership is the most instructive brand in the peer set for community-building and programming strategy. Their referral mechanics (22%/22% via Perkville), class format variety, and breathwork extension all have direct SOUNDBATH analogues worth examining. The key differentiator SOUNDBATH has over Othership is the cultural programming layer — artist talks, residencies, events. That's the space Othership doesn't occupy.

On the tech side: Mariana Tek should be evaluated for the Experience Stack as a direct Glofox alternative. If SOUNDBATH runs any class-based or programmed sessions, Mariana Tek's scheduling and branded app model may be a better fit than Glofox, which skews toward gym memberships. Their Perkville implementation is the clearest example in the peer set of a working referral-to-booking pipeline.

Business model

Bathhouse runs an open-access circuit model — day passes, treatment bookings (massage + hammam scrub), and recurring memberships. Critically, they are not class-based: guests check in and self-navigate the pools, saunas, and steam rooms at their own pace, with guided Aufguss sessions (sauna rituals) offered on a schedule but included with entry. Revenue streams include day passes (starting $39), treatments (~$154–$204), memberships ($145–$225/mo), a multi-visit pack option, gift cards, a restaurant, and a rooftop pool with a bar. The rooftop is a significant premium product — reserved daybeds and lounge chairs command additional fees. A rewards program and a "Give 25%, Get 25%" referral program are both live. They also built an AI Search feature (/ai-search) — notable for a wellness brand at this scale.

try.be — what it is and why it matters

Given that Bathhouse — the biggest operator in the space — uses it at scale across two NYC locations, it deserves evaluation alongside Glofox and Zenoti for Phase 2–3. Key advantage over class scheduling tools: it is designed for timed-entry and capacity-controlled spaces, not class-based scheduling.

Implications for SOUNDBATH

Bathhouse is the revenue and scale blueprint. Their open-circuit model, multi-revenue architecture (day pass + treatments + membership + F&B), and timed-entry booking approach map closely to where SOUNDBATH wants to be at Phase 3. Their adoption of try.be as the booking layer is the clearest signal that class-scheduling platforms (Mariana Tek, Glofox) may not be the right fit for an open-circuit, capacity-controlled bathhouse — try.be deserves a proper evaluation call.

Their AI Search implementation is worth watching — even a simple LLM-powered FAQ reduces front-desk load and improves conversion on the booking page. This is table-stakes within 18 months for any premium wellness brand.

The community gap is SOUNDBATH's clearest differentiator against Bathhouse. They have no community platform, no cultural programming, and no brand identity beyond recovery. SOUNDBATH's social-forward, culturally-rooted positioning occupies meaningfully different territory.

What this peer set tells us
Community is unoccupied whitespace

None of the three competitors — including the most socially-positioned brand in the space (Othership) — have an owned community platform. Othership has culture, events, and breathwork programming but no digital gathering space between visits. Bathhouse has none. Grotto has none. A pre-opening founding member community that is active before doors open is uncontested in this peer set.

No one has a CRM spine

All three competitors have customer data fragmented across booking platforms, ticketing tools, and email lists with no central record of who a guest is, where they came from, or how they've engaged over time. That's the structural gap. Whoever builds a clean HubSpot CRM with proper source attribution from day one will outperform on retention and marketing efficiency within 12 months.

Referral benchmark is 22–25%

Both operating competitors use 22–25% referral discounts — Othership via Perkville (confirmed), Bathhouse natively. This is the market-established incentive level. SOUNDBATH's referral-to-booking pipeline should either match this range or differentiate with access-based incentives (founding member priority, early community entry) rather than pure discount.

Grotto is the urgency signal

Grotto Baths opens Summer 2026 in Wynwood — same market, same timing, with a team that includes hotel operators from Standard and Faena. They have zero digital infrastructure visible right now. SOUNDBATH going live with a founding member pipeline and an active community before Grotto opens is a real, time-limited window that closes when Grotto launches.

My recommendation · Based on this analysis
The Experience Stack.
Here is exactly why the competitor landscape points there.
1
Othership — the benchmark social wellness brand — has no community platform. Circle.so is in the Experience Stack.
Othership is the most culturally relevant social bathhouse operating right now. They have breathwork classes, events, comedy nights, and a strong Instagram presence. What they don't have is a digital space where members gather between visits. Circle.so fills that gap for SOUNDBATH. The Architect Stack doesn't include it. For a brand whose entire brief is built around social connection, launching without the only community tool in the peer set would be the single biggest missed opportunity in this build.
2
Grotto opens Summer 2026 in Wynwood. Beehiiv lets SOUNDBATH build a founding member audience before Grotto has a booking link.
Grotto is the most direct competitive threat in Miami — same neighborhood energy, strong hotel-operations team, alcohol-free F&B, Summer 2026 opening. Their current digital footprint is nearly zero. The window to establish SOUNDBATH as Miami's wellness brand of record — before Grotto enters the conversation — is open right now and it is finite. Beehiiv is the fastest tool available for turning that window into a real subscriber list. The Architect Stack uses HubSpot email sequences instead, which are more powerful post-launch but slower to activate pre-launch. Beehiiv wins the founding member phase, and that phase happens once.
3
Bathhouse NYC — the biggest operator in the space — has a fragmented stack and no CRM. HubSpot is in both stacks. SOUNDBATH wins the data game regardless.
Bathhouse runs Squarespace, try.be, Shopify, and Google Cloud in separate systems with no central customer record. They're doing $7M+ revenue and 1,000 guests per day in NYC with zero visible CRM. HubSpot in the Experience Stack gives SOUNDBATH a structural data advantage over the biggest peer in this set from day one. This isn't a reason to choose Experience over Architect — it's a reason to confirm that both stacks close a gap that no competitor has closed. The Experience Stack gets there faster because Glofox and Beehiiv have cleaner HubSpot paths than Zenoti and Webflow.
4
No competitor has built a referral-to-booking pipeline. The Experience Stack does. That 22–25% market referral rate is sitting uncaptured in Miami.
Othership uses Perkville — a third-party referral bolt-on — to run their 22% referral program. Bathhouse appears to run theirs natively in try.be. Neither has a clean referral → newsletter → booking attribution chain. The Experience Stack's Beehiiv referral flow, tracked through HubSpot source fields back to Glofox, is a closed-loop pipeline that none of these competitors have. In a Miami market where Grotto is coming and word-of-mouth is the primary acquisition channel for social wellness, that pipeline is the acquisition engine.
Where the Architect Stack still has a case

Bathhouse is scaling to 7 locations. If SOUNDBATH has multi-city ambitions on a 2–3 year horizon, the Architect Stack's Stripe billing ownership and Webflow CMS portability are more durable long-term. Glofox contracts can create lock-in, and Beehiiv is not a CRM — it's a newsletter platform. The Experience Stack is optimized for winning Miami. The Architect Stack is optimized for owning the infrastructure when Miami becomes the first of several cities.

These stacks are not mutually exclusive. Start with Experience. If the multi-city conversation is serious by the end of Phase 2, migrate Glofox billing to Stripe before Phase 3. The community layer and CRM spine transfer regardless.
