The three decisions that matter most

The billing decision: Glofox vs. Stripe
Experience Stack · Glofox

Convenient, fast, wellness-native

Glofox was built for this business type. Membership tiers, branded app, recurring billing, and class booking come pre-configured. A non-technical team can manage it day-to-day. Important caveats verified through user review research: no public pricing, multiple reports of hidden fees, unexpected price increases, and pressure toward long-term (3-year) contracts. Get a detailed written quote, confirm all fees, and verify month-to-month availability before signing.

Architect Stack · Stripe

Portable, powerful, developer-required

Stripe is the most portable billing infrastructure that exists. Payment history, subscription logic, and customer IDs live independently. If any future ops platform is replaced, billing continuity is fully protected. Real cost: Stripe's 2.9%+30¢ per transaction — at 100 founding members paying $150/mo, that's ~$450/mo in processing fees. Requires real developer configuration to implement membership tiers and billing flows.

Honest call

Validate Glofox's contract terms before committing — or use Stripe from the start

The Experience Stack is faster and easier. But before choosing Glofox, get full pricing in writing, confirm month-to-month terms are available, and verify data export capabilities. If Glofox's terms are acceptable, it's the right Phase 2 choice for speed. If the contract is multi-year or pricing is opaque, go directly to Stripe. The hybrid approach: use Glofox short-term, plan a Stripe migration once the business stabilizes, and add the glofox_member_id field to HubSpot now so the migration has a clean target.

The community decision: Circle.so vs. HubSpot email
Experience Stack · Circle.so

A real destination for founding members

Circle.so creates a branded community that founding members can actually inhabit before the bathhouse opens — discussions, programming previews, and member-to-member connection. For Miami's creative, social audience, this is a genuine brand differentiator. It gives SOUNDBATH a community story before a single tile is laid. The Professional plan at $89/mo (annual) includes a 2% transaction fee on payments processed through Circle — factor this into membership payment flow planning.

Architect Stack · HubSpot email

Functional, not experiential

HubSpot email sequences are effective for nurturing contacts and sending updates. But they're one-directional — members receive information, they don't participate. For a brand explicitly built around social immersion and cultural programming, this undersells the product before it opens. The $89/mo saving is real. But the lost pre-opening community momentum — measured in missed founding member referrals and word-of-mouth — likely costs more.

Honest call

Circle.so is worth adding to either stack for this brand

The Architect Stack can add Circle.so without architectural conflict — it's an additive layer that syncs to HubSpot via Zapier. The data ownership model is unaffected. SOUNDBATH's positioning is explicitly social and cultural. A pre-opening Circle.so community does three things: gives founding members something to be part of immediately, generates organic word-of-mouth, and gives Vishal real signal on what programming his audience wants before opening day. Both stacks should seriously consider including it.

The Phase 3 decision: Book4Time vs. Zenoti
Experience Stack · Book4Time

The hotel spa industry standard

Used by Four Seasons, Mandarin Oriental, and most major hotel spa brands in North America. The credible choice if hotel partnership at Phase 3 is a realistic possibility. Strong North American support presence and mature API. The right choice if SOUNDBATH benchmarks itself against the hospitality tier or pursues a hotel integration.

Architect Stack · Zenoti

Purpose-built for standalone wellness

More feature-complete for a standalone wellness business — deeper membership logic, better POS integration, stronger native forms and consent workflows. More modern API with cleaner HubSpot and Stripe integration paths. Used by Waldorf Astoria Spa, Ritz-Carlton Spa. Better choice if SOUNDBATH is definitively standalone with no hotel partnership planned.

Honest call

Start conversations with both now — decide at site selection

The right choice depends on whether hotel partnership is on the table. Neither platform should be committed to before the physical site is confirmed. Start both sales processes now — 4–5 months before target opening date. The demos will reveal which platform's UX, support, and integration story fits the actual operations team. Both require 3+ month onboarding cycles. Do not leave this decision to 60 days before opening.

Who should choose which

Choose the Experience Stack if...
  • The waitlist page needs to be live within 2 weeks
  • A branded pre-opening community is central to the brand story
  • The team operating it day-to-day is not technical
  • Miami's creative class seeing a polished UX from first contact is the priority
  • Glofox's contract terms are acceptable after due diligence
  • Hotel partnership at Phase 3 is a realistic possibility
Choose the Architect Stack if...
  • Data ownership and billing portability are genuinely non-negotiable
  • A longer Phase 1 is acceptable in exchange for cleaner architecture
  • A developer stays engaged long-term to maintain Stripe billing logic
  • SOUNDBATH may expand to multiple locations in a 3–5 year horizon
  • The community experience pre-opening is less central (or Circle.so is added separately)
  • Standalone wellness is the permanent model — no hotel partnership

The honest recommendation: a deliberate hybrid

Use the Experience Stack for Phase 1 and Phase 2 — Framer, Beehiiv, Circle.so, and Luma get the brand launched beautifully and fast, with a genuine pre-opening community that fits the Miami audience. Add Circle.so regardless of which stack is chosen — the $89/mo is worth it for this brand specifically. On billing: validate Glofox's contract terms thoroughly before signing. If terms are month-to-month and data export is confirmed, use Glofox for Phase 2 speed. If the contract is multi-year or pricing is opaque, go directly to Stripe. Add stripe_customer_id to HubSpot contacts from day one regardless of stack — so a future migration has a clean home. At Phase 3: start Book4Time and Zenoti demos simultaneously now. The right choice depends on site type. Neither decision should be deferred to 60 days before opening.
