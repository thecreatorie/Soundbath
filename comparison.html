03

The three decisions that matter most

Billing: Glofox vs. Stripe
Experience Stack · Glofox

Convenient, fast, wellness-native

Glofox was built for this business type. Membership tiers, branded app, recurring billing, and class booking come pre-configured. A non-technical team can manage it day-to-day after initial setup. Critical due diligence: no public pricing, multiple independent reports of hidden fees, unexpected price increases, and pressure toward multi-year contracts. Get a full written quote, confirm month-to-month availability, and verify data export before signing anything.

Architect Stack · Stripe

Portable, powerful, developer-required

Stripe is the most portable billing infrastructure available. Payment history, subscription logic, and customer IDs live independently of any other platform. If any future ops tool is replaced, billing continuity is fully protected. Requires real developer configuration — subscription tiers, webhooks, and member portal UX are not self-service. Transaction fees apply per charge — model these against projected membership volume to understand the true cost at scale.

Honest call

Validate Glofox's contract before committing — or use Stripe from the start

The Experience Stack is faster and easier. But before choosing Glofox, get full pricing in writing, confirm month-to-month terms are available, and verify data export capabilities. If Glofox's terms are clean, it's the right Phase 2 choice. If the contract is restrictive or pricing is opaque, go directly to Stripe from the start — it takes more setup but the billing layer is fully owned from day one. Either way, establish the tool ID fields in HubSpot contacts immediately so any future migration has a clean target.

Community: Circle.so vs. HubSpot email
Experience Stack · Circle.so

A real destination for founding members

Circle.so creates a branded community that founding members can actually inhabit before the bathhouse opens — discussions, programming previews, and member-to-member connection. For Miami's creative, social audience, this is a genuine differentiator. It gives SOUNDBATH a community story to tell before a single tile is laid. Note that Circle charges a transaction fee on payments processed through the platform — plan membership payment flows accordingly.

Architect Stack · HubSpot email

Functional, not experiential

HubSpot email sequences are effective for nurturing contacts and sending updates. But they're one-directional — members receive information, they don't participate. For a brand explicitly built around social immersion and cultural programming, this undersells the product before it opens. The cost savings are real, but the lost pre-opening community momentum — measured in missed founding member referrals and word-of-mouth — likely costs more over time.

Honest call

Circle.so is worth adding to either stack for this brand

The Architect Stack can add Circle.so without architectural conflict — it's an additive layer that syncs to HubSpot via Zapier without replacing either Stripe or Zenoti. The data ownership model is unaffected. SOUNDBATH's positioning is explicitly social and cultural. A pre-opening Circle.so community does three things: gives founding members something to belong to immediately, generates organic word-of-mouth, and gives real signal on what programming the audience wants before opening day. Both stacks should seriously consider including it.

Phase 3 ops: Book4Time vs. Zenoti
Experience Stack · Book4Time

Established, mature, widely trusted

Used by Four Seasons, Marriott, Hilton, and Hyatt. Now operating as Book4Time by Agilysys following its $150M acquisition in August 2024 — same product, now with Agilysys's broader hospitality PMS ecosystem behind it. Strong North American support presence, mature scheduling logic, and a well-documented API. Has been in the market long enough that most implementation edge cases are known and solved. The more conservative choice of the two.

Architect Stack · Zenoti

Modern, flexible, deeper wellness logic

More feature-complete for a standalone wellness business — deeper membership management, better native POS integration, stronger consent form workflows. More modern API that connects more cleanly to HubSpot and Stripe. Used by MGM Resort Spa and large multi-location wellness chains. The better choice for a business that expects its operations to evolve quickly.

The call

Run demos with both before the physical site is confirmed

Both platforms require 3–4 month onboarding cycles — start conversations now regardless of which direction feels right. The demos will reveal which platform's UX, support model, and day-to-day management experience is the better fit for the actual operations team. Neither should be committed to before site selection is finalized and the physical layout is known.

04

Who should choose which

Choose the Experience Stack if...
  • The waitlist page needs to be live within 2 weeks
  • A branded pre-opening community is central to the brand strategy
  • The team operating it day-to-day is not technical
  • The Miami creative class seeing a polished UX from first visit is the priority
  • Glofox's contract terms are acceptable after full due diligence
Choose the Architect Stack if...
  • Data ownership and billing portability are genuinely non-negotiable
  • A longer Phase 1 is acceptable in exchange for cleaner long-term architecture
  • Developer engagement continues beyond launch — ongoing involvement is expected
  • The community layer can come later or Circle.so is added as a separate decision
