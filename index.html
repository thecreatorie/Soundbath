Building SOUNDBATH
from the ground up.

Two architecture approaches for a social-forward bathhouse and cultural space in Miami — a membership-driven community that needs to capture early interest now, activate founding memberships before opening, and integrate smoothly with bathhouse operations at launch. Prepared by Caroline Godwin.

Two approaches — explore each or read the full comparison
The Experience Stack

Built for speed, luxury feel, and community from day one.

Purpose-built wellness tools, a polished member app, and a curated community platform. The fastest path from brief to live. Best when brand experience and pre-opening community are top priorities.

01

What both stacks agree on

These decisions are non-negotiable regardless of which strategy is chosen.

Cloudflare for DNS

Free CDN, DDoS protection, DNSSEC. All domains point here regardless of registrar.

Google Workspace

Business email, MFA, Shared Drives. SPF + DKIM + DMARC authentication before any outbound email.

HubSpot as CRM spine

The master relationship record. Every customer-facing tool syncs back here. Free CRM is genuinely capable to start.

Zapier as early glue

Lightweight integration layer between tools. Graduate to direct API webhooks when complexity justifies it.

Looker Studio for analytics

Free unified dashboard connecting all operational tools. Sufficient for early-stage reporting.

Data portability first

Confirm full customer data export via CSV or API before signing any vendor contract. Non-negotiable.

Domain strategy

One canonical URL, everything else 301 redirects. Acquire soundbath.miami immediately.

Start Phase 3 conversations now

Book4Time and Zenoti both require 3–4 month procurement cycles. Begin vendor conversations early.

02

Three phases, clear milestones

Both strategies follow the same phased structure — the tool choices within each phase differ.

PhaseGoalWhenKey outputs
Phase 1 — Foundation Establish digital backbone. Capture early demand. Launch waitlist. Now DNS, email, landing page, CRM, waitlist live
Phase 2 — Membership Activate founding memberships and programming. Build pre-opening community. 6–9 months pre-opening Memberships live, community active, events ticketed
Phase 3 — Operations Full bathhouse operational layer — booking, check-in, POS, RFID, analytics. 3–4 months before opening End-to-end ops stack live and integrated
